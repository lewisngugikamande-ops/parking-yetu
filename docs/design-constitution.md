# Parking Yetu — Design Constitution

## Preamble

Parking Yetu exists to move people and vehicles through every gate safely, quickly, and with complete confidence.

## Article I — The Operator

**1.1 — The operator always comes first.**

**1.2 — Every second matters.**

**1.3 — Reduce uncertainty.**

## Article II — The Interface

**2.1 — Beauty serves clarity.**

**2.2 — The interface is quiet until it has something to say.**

**2.3 — Every color has a job.**

**2.4 — Movement has meaning.**

## Article III — The Experience

**3.1 — Trust is earned through consistency.**

**3.2 — One glance, one understanding.**

**3.3 — Every interaction should reduce uncertainty.**

**3.4 — Mistakes are reversible.**

## Article IV — The Technology

**4.1 — The operator never waits.**

**4.2 — Every second has an owner.**

**4.3 — One source of truth.**

**4.4 — Human before database.**

## Article V — The Brand

**5.1 — Calm is a feature.**

**5.2 — Movement. Managed. Trusted.**

**5.3 — Technology should never become another gate.**

## Article VI — The Evolution

**6.1 — Restore before redesign.**

**6.2 — Scale, don't remove.**

**6.3 — Accessibility is non-negotiable.**

## Article VII — Operational Truth

**7.1 — Reality is the source of truth.**

**7.2 — Time is evidence.**

**7.3 — Every movement leaves a trace.**

## Article VIII — Engineering Principles

**8.1 — Simplicity before cleverness.**

**8.2 — Build today's abstraction tomorrow.**

**8.3 — Refactor in small steps.**

**8.4 — Every dependency must earn its place.**

## Article IX — Respect the Operator

**9.1 — Never blame the user.**

**9.2 — Make success obvious.**

**9.3 — Make failure recoverable.**

**9.4 — Confidence is part of usability.**

---

## The Four Permanent Values

Speed → We remove delay.
Clarity → We remove confusion.
Trust → We remove doubt.
Continuity → The operation never stops.

---

## The Parking Yetu Oath

We build software that operators trust.

We choose clarity over cleverness.

We choose reliability over novelty.

We measure success by confidence, not by complexity.

Every line of code should make movement easier, operations safer, and people more confident.

---

**Movement. Managed. Trusted.**

---

## Article VIII — Every Screen Has One Job

**8.1 — Login does one thing. Authenticate.**

**8.2 — Workstation does one thing. Move vehicles.**

**8.3 — Reports do one thing. Answer questions.**

**8.4 — Settings do one thing. Configure.**

If a screen starts doing multiple jobs, split it.

---

## Article IX — Confidence Above All

**9.1 — Every improvement must make the operator feel more confident.**

**9.2 — If confidence decreases, even if the software becomes technically better, the change is rejected.**

**9.3 — A 12-hour shift without questioning the software is success.**
